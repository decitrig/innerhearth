About Us

Inner Hearth Yoga is the place to stoke up your yoga practice along with the Point Breeze community! Whether you're a novice or a longtime practitioner, our skilled and attentive teachers at the Hearth will meet you where you are. Don't worry about having the right outfit, the best attitude, or the perfect pose. Inner Hearth's yoga is for every body!

Our teachers help students recognize their bodies' potential through precise and compassionate instruction, supplying the anatomical context students need.

Class Descriptions

Aligned Flow

A continually flowing series of Sun Salutations and postures connected with the breath, spiced with relevant alignment cues. Students should be in good health, and able to move continuously for 20 to 30 minutes.

Yoga For Beginners

An ongoing 6-week series for students with little to no yoga experience or those who want a refresher on yoga fundamentals. Emphasis on learning essential yoga postures, anatomical landmarks of the body, and how to move through a Sun Salutation.

Yoga I

Emphasis on refining essential yoga postures, building strength and endurance through Sun Salutations and standing poses, and introducing some revolved poses and backbends. We will begin to explore arm strengthening poses as preparation for inversions.

Yoga II

For students with a current yoga practice, whether at home or at a studio. Emphasis is on exploring creative flows within Sun Salutations, building strength and endurance with strong standing poses and variations, and refining backbends, twists and inversions. Classes are sequenced to open and strengthen the body in preparation for a specific pose or category of poses.

Yin Yoga

Yin yoga is a quiet practice where students hold supported postures on the floor for longer periods of time to target the connective tissues (ligaments, bones, fascia) of the hips, pelvis, spine and shoulders. It is a wonderful compliment to an active lifestyle and appropriate for those who are living with chronic pain or injury. All levels are welcome!

Yin Yogassage™

During a Yin Yogassage class, students of all levels will be led through the quiet, reflective practice of Yin Yoga while receiving massage as they rest in the poses. Students with chronic conditions are welcome, and will be assisted in modifying their practice to accommodate their conditions. Class size is limited, insuring that each student receives generous attention. Preregistration is REQUIRED for Yin Yogassage. You can register online

Baby And Me

Get back on your mat-- with your baby! The Baby and Me Series gives you a chance to reacquaint yourself with your body and connect with other new parents. Expect lots of interaction, eye contact and play with your baby during class. We know (and everybody else in the class knows) that babies cry, diapers need to be changed, and sometimes you need to nurse or give a bottle. Heck, we don't even care if you've had a shower in days! New parents need some yoga. Fathers are welcome any time. Mothers are welcome after a 6 week post-partum checkup goes well! The practice playfully incorporates baby while teaching caregivers the gentle art of infant massage.

Slow Flow

Half mindful movement, half restorative practice. This class will wake up, stretch and strengthen your body first with active poses, allowing you to relax completely during the last half of the class in restorative poses done with the support of props.

Prenatal Yoga

Come relieve tension and build strength strength with a community of mothers both new and experienced! Strengthen the muscles that support a healthy birth, relieve lower back and shoulder strain, and learn to calm your mind in preparation for one of life's greatest transitions. Don't worry if you've never done yoga before, and it doesn't matter where you are in your pregnancy! Wear comfortable clothes that don't grab at your belly, bring a mat if you have one—or use one of ours. This is for women with typical (not high risk) pregnancies. When in doubt, check with your doctor or midwife!

Happy Hour Yoga

A short, fast-flowing, fun class to help you transition from your workweek into the weekend with a light heart and an open body. Students should be in good health, and able to move continuously for 20 to 30 minutes.

Early Bird Flow

Wake up and move! This class is designed to get you up, moving and centered before the work of the day begins. Students should be in good health, and able to move continuously for 20 to 30 minutes.

Pricing

How Our Classes Work

Inner Hearth Yoga offers classes in sessions, like semesters! Just like a class offered at a university, each weekly class builds on what was taught during previous classes. This progressive teaching style helps students grow strength, skill and flexibility throughout the session.

When you register for a session at Inner Hearth:

  • You get a better deal, because your per-class cost decreases.
  • You get the best of both worlds - attend all the classes you can, but make up all the classes you miss! You may make up classes either before or after you miss them, in any class that's appropriate for your level (with the exception of Yin Yogassage), by the end of the session in which you missed the class.
  • Regular attendance to classes not only builds your practice but builds a strong, supportive yoga community. Making a commitment to your practice means making a commitment to you.
  • Your teachers gets to know you better, making it easier for them to offer modifications that are suited just for you

We offer a $5 preview week before each new session starts, giving you the chance to try out different teachers, class times, and class types. You can register online or in person at the studio at any point during the session - we will pro-rate your session fee based off of how many weeks have elapsed when you decide to register.

We also offer Six-Week Short Courses. These are like taking a special workshop that meets once a week for six weeks! We offer them on topics we think you’ll like, such as Yoga Basics for Beginners and Post-Natal Yoga for moms and pre-crawling babies. You can get a taste of the short course content by coming to $5 Preview Week - try before you buy!

Our Teachers

Jill Cummings (co-owner, E-RYT/LCSW)

Jill Cummings found yoga back in 1999 at the 12th Street Gym in Philadelphia while seeking a practice to nurture her body after years of banging it up as an athlete. A few years later she became certified to teach through the thoughtful and compassionate teachings of Corina Benner and Jill Manning at Wake Up Yoga. She also completed trainings in kids', prenatal and postpartum yoga with Gail Silver of Yoga Child. She's also spiced up her teachings by studying Yin yoga with Corina Benner, yoga anatomy with Paul Grilley, Sanskrit with Manorama and has practiced with many more amazing teachers. Jill formerly co-founded and taught at Yogawood in Collingswood, NJ until her family relocated to Pittsburgh in 2009. While independently teaching the most amazing students in da ‘burgh she also co-created Yin YogassageTM with Brad Mumpower in 2011. Jill then met Lauren at another local yoga studio…and the rest is history!

Her teachings are infused with anatomical and physiological considerations , compassion and attention for each unique student , a lot of laughter and tons of fun! When not teaching yoga, Jill is currently finishing her Master of Occupational Therapy at the University of Pittsburgh, hanging with her husband and 2 kooky kids, goofy hound dog, Charlie and crazy cat, Kitty.

Lauren Sims (co-owner, RYT)

In 2004, Lauren Sims accidentally walked into a yoga class instead of a martial arts class and never walked out. Lauren is passionate about helping students realize their capacity for growth and change, and believes that the most profound possibility yoga offers is the chance to become an active, joyful participant in your own life.

After years of study at Willow Street Yoga in Takoma Park, MD, Lauren graduated from their comprehensive teacher training program led by Suzie Hurley and Maria Hamburger in 2010. Since then, she's studied therapeutic yoga and anatomy with Jenny Otto of Body Balance Yoga, completed a prenatal teacher training with Janice Clarfield, and continues to study with master teachers such as Douglas Brooks, Christina Sell, Bernadette Birney, and Emma Magenta. Her classes inspire the body to reach its full potential through mindful sequencing and precise instruction, and are infused with warmth, humor, and just a bit of tough love!

Lauren moved to Pittsburgh in June 2012, and fell in love with the City of Steel at first sight. She lives in Shadyside with her husband and two entitled cats.

Kim Eckel

Kim Eckelfirst turned to yoga in the late 90s to complement her time spent running competitively in college. It did not take long for her to discover the wonderful peace that can come through practice and in 2006, she completed her 200 hour certification at Kripalu. Kim aims to cultivate compassionate self-awareness among her students, as well as an appreciation for the equanimity that can be found through practice on the mat. Her classes vary depending on the needs of her students; however, she continually seeks to provide a safe, fun and relaxing environment. When not practicing yoga or running, Kim can be found working on her PhD and enjoying time with her husband and son.

Brad Mumpower

Brad Mumpower is a Certified Massage Therapist and Reiki Master working in private practice in Pittsburgh, specializing in deep tissue and trigger point therapy. In addition to teaching at the Pittsburgh School of Massage, Brad runs a small wellness center with a group of massage therapists and yoga instructors. In 2011, Brad's 12 years of bodywork experience lead him to collaborate with Jill Cummings to create Yin Yogassage™, a practice that seamlessly blends restorative yoga poses and bodywork.

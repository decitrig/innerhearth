About Us

Inner Hearth Yoga is the place to stoke up your yoga practice alongside the Point Breeze community! Whether you're a novice or a longtime practitioner, our skilled and attentive teachers at the Hearth will meet you where you are. Don't worry about having the right outfit, the best attitude, or the perfect pose. Inner Hearth's yoga is for every body!

Our teachers help students recognize their bodies' potential through precise but compassionate instruction, and supply the anatomical context students need to make empowered choices about their yoga practice.

Class Descriptions

Aligned Flow

A continually flowing series of Sun Salutations and postures connected with the breath, spiced with relevant alignment cues. Students should be in good health, and able to move continuously for 20 to 30 minutes without a rest.

Yoga For Beginners

An ongoing 6-week series for students with little to no yoga experience. Emphasis on learning essential yoga postures, anatomical landmarks of the body, and how to move through a Sun Salutation.

Yoga I

Emphasis on refining essential yoga postures, building endurance through Sun Salutations and standing poses, and introducing some revolved poses and backbends. We will begin to explore arm strengthening poses in preparation for inversions.

Yoga II

For students with a current yoga practice, whether at home or at a studio. Emphasis is on exploring creative flows within Sun Salutations, building endurance with strong standing poses and variations, and refining backbends, twists and inversions. Classes are sequenced to open and strengthen the body in preparation for a specific pose or category of poses.

Yin Yogassage™

During a Yin Yogassage class, students of all levels will be lead through the quiet, reflective practice of Yin Yoga while receiving massage as they rest in the poses. Students with chronic conditions are welcome, and will be assisted in modifying their practice to accommodate their conditions. Class size is limited so that each student receives generous attention. Preregistration is REQUIRED for Yin Yogassage.

Post-Natal Yoga Series

For mothers 4 weeks to 2 years post-partum, Post-Natal yoga focuses on helping new mamas find strength and balance in bodies after baby. Post-Natal yoga offers a sustained focus on opening areas that grow tired from breast- and bottle-feeding, recovering strength and tone in the pelvic floor and abdomen, and resting and restoring tired bodies and minds. This series also focuses on building a supportive community of new mothers.

Happy Hour Yoga

A short, fast-flowing, fun class to help you transition from your workweek into the weekend with a light heart and an open body. Students should be comfortable moving through several Sun Salutations without pain or discomfort.

Early Bird Flow

Wake up and move! This class is designed to get you up, moving and centered before the work of the day begins. Students should be comfortable moving through several Sun Salutations without pain or discomfort.

Pay What You Can

This class is open to anyone who has a restricted income but still wants to practice yoga under the guidance of a skilled teacher. Pay what you can afford, and enjoy a great practice!

Our Teachers

Julie Menge

Julie Menge started teaching yoga in 2010 when she completed her 200 hour teacher training in the style of vinyasa yoga. After much soul searching, Julie left her career in human resources to teach yoga full-time and hasn't looked back since. She hopes that you will walk away from her classes with strength, calmness, and a sense of empowerment regarding your practice, your life, and the decisions you make on and off the mat. Julie has also studied and teaches yin yoga. She loves encouraging her vinyasa students to check out the "other half of their yoga practice" and try a yin class!

Julie's classes are mainly influenced by Seane Corn (after completing a teacher training intensive with her in 2012), as well as the many incredibly talented local teachers in Pittsburgh. As of January 2013, she is also enrolled in a YogaWorks teacher training program to continue her yoga education. Julie lives in Wilkinsburg with her husband Nate and their rescued dogs, Chelsea and Buzz.

Lauren Sims

In 2004, Lauren Sims accidentally walked into a yoga class instead of a martial arts class and never walked out. Lauren is passionate about helping students realize their innate capacity for growth and change, and believes that the most profound possibility yoga offers is the chance to become an active, joyful participant in your own life.

After years of study at Willow Street Yoga in Takoma Park, MD, Lauren graduated from their comprehensive teacher training program led by Suzie Hurley and Maria Hamburger in 2010. Since then, she's studied therapeutic yoga and anatomy with Jenny Otto of Body Balance Yoga, completed a prenatal teacher training with Janice Clarfield, and continues to study with master teachers such as Douglas Brooks, Christina Sell, Sally Kempton, Zhenja LaRosa, and Martin Kirk. Her classes aspire to allow the body to reach its full potential through mindful sequencing and precise instruction, and are infused with warmth, humor, and just a bit of tough love!

Lauren moved to Pittsburgh in June 2012, and fell in love with the City of Steel at first sight. She lives in Shadyside with her husband and two entitled cats.
